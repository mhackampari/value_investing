## Framework

Every company report must address these four pillars in depth:

---

## 1. Moat Analysis

Identify the *source* of competitive advantage — not just that one exists:
- **Cost advantage**: Can they produce cheaper than peers structurally?
- **Switching costs**: How painful is it for customers to leave?
- **Network effects**: Does the product get more valuable as more people use it?
- **Intangible assets**: Patents, licenses, brand that commands pricing power
- **Efficient scale**: Natural monopoly or duopoly in a niche market

Assess moat **durability**: Is it widening, stable, or eroding? What threatens it?

---

## 2. Risk Analysis

Be explicit and specific — avoid generic risks:
- **Business risks**: Disruption, customer concentration, regulatory, commodity input exposure
- **Financial risks**: Debt levels, refinancing risk, pension obligations, off-balance-sheet liabilities
- **Management risks**: Incentive misalignment, empire building, related-party transactions
- **Valuation risks**: What assumptions must hold for current price to be justified?
- **Macro risks**: Only include if directly material to the thesis (e.g., FX exposure for international earners)

Use a **bear case** framing: if the thesis is wrong, how wrong can it be?

---

## 3. Catalysts

What will cause the market to recognize value over your investment horizon?
- Near-term (0–12 months): earnings inflections, contract wins, product launches
- Medium-term (1–3 years): market expansion, margin normalization, capital returns
- Long-term (3+ years): compounding of reinvestment, TAM penetration

Distinguish between **value-creating catalysts** (grow intrinsic value) and **value-recognition catalysts** (close the discount to intrinsic value).

---

## 4. Valuation

Use multiple methods and triangulate:
- **DCF**: State your assumptions explicitly (FCF growth, discount rate, terminal multiple). Run a base, bull, and bear scenario.
- **Earnings power value (EPV)**: Value the business assuming zero growth — floor valuation.
- **Comparable multiples**: P/E, EV/EBIT, EV/FCF vs. peers and historical range. Contextualize *why* a premium or discount is warranted.
- **Owner earnings**: Buffett's preferred metric — net income + D&A – maintenance capex
- **Reverse DCF**: What growth rate is implied by the current price? Is that realistic?

Always state: **intrinsic value range**, **current price**, **implied upside/downside**, and **margin of safety**.

---

## Key Financial Metrics

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) | Core measure of business quality |
| ROIC vs. WACC spread | Value creation test |
| Free Cash Flow Yield | True earnings power |
| Gross / Operating / FCF margins (trend) | Pricing power and efficiency |
| Net debt / EBITDA | Financial leverage |
| Revenue and earnings growth (5–10yr) | Track record |
| Payout ratio / buyback yield | Capital return discipline |
| Insider ownership | Alignment |
